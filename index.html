Y2K

Retro-Futurism & The Millennium Bug

The Millennium Bug

Originally, **Y2K** stood for the "Year 2000 Problem." Computer programmers in the 20th century often used two-digit codes for years (e.g., "99" for 1999) to save memory. There was a global fear that when the clock struck midnight on Jan 1, 2000, computers would interpret "00" as 1900, causing global chaos.

The Aesthetic

Today, Y2K refers to a specific visual style (1998–2003). It celebrates:

  • Clear Tech: Translucent electronics like the iMac G3.
  • Cyber Graphics: Neon gradients, 3D blobs, and metallic textures.
  • Optimism: A bright, tech-positive view of the future.
